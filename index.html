Tense / Structure What it is Formula Example
PAST TENSES
Past SimpleCompleted action at a specific past timeS + V-ed / irregularShe left school at 6pm.
Past ContinuousAction in progress at a past momentS + was/were + V-ingHe was sitting on the porch.
Past PerfectAction completed before another past eventS + had + past participleI saw she had left already.
Past Perfect ContinuousOngoing action up to a past pointS + had been + V-ingShe had been teaching for 5 years.
PRESENT TENSES
Present SimpleHabits, facts, statesS + V / S + V-sShe works in London.
Present ContinuousAction happening now or temp. arrangementS + am/is/are + V-ingI am going to the dentist tomorrow.
Present PerfectPast action with present relevance / experienceS + have/has + past participleI have heard many stories.
Present Perfect ContinuousAction ongoing from past to nowS + have/has been + V-ingShe has been teaching since 2019.
FUTURE FORMS
will + infinitivePrediction / spontaneous decisionS + will + VI think I will stay here.
be going toPlanned intention / evidence-based predictionS + am/is/are going to + VShe is going to do a training course.
Future ContinuousAction in progress at a future momentS + will be + V-ingThis time next week I will be lying on a beach.
CONDITIONALS
First ConditionalReal / likely future situationIf + Present Simple, will + VIf I find it, I will bring it to you.
Second ConditionalUnreal / hypothetical present or futureIf + Past Simple, would + VIf I won the lottery, I would travel.
Time ClausesFuture meaning after when/before/as soon as/untilconjunction + Present SimpleCall me when dinner is ready.
PASSIVES
Passive VoiceSubject receives the actionS + be (correct tense) + past participleOil has been discovered near the islands.
Modal PassiveModal + passive infinitivemodal + be + past participlePhotos must not be taken here.
MODALS
must / can'tLogical deduction (present)must/can't + be/VHe must be hungry. She can't be there.
must have / can't haveLogical deduction (past)must/can't + have + past participleHe must have left already.
might / could + havePast possibility / speculationmight/could + have + past participleShe could have phoned while I was out.
REPORTED SPEECH
Reported StatementsBackshift: present→past, will→would, can→couldsaid (that) + backshifted clause"I'm fine" → She said she was fine.
Reported QuestionsStatement word order, no auxiliary inversionasked + wh-/if + subject + verb"Where do you live?" → asked where she lived.
Reported CommandsReporting verb + object + to-infinitivetold/asked/warned + object + (not) to VShe asked him to open the window.
VERB PATTERNS
Gerund (-ing)After: avoid, enjoy, suggest, before, afterverb + V-ingShe avoided making eye contact.
Infinitive (to-V)After: try, want, decide, in order, manageverb + to + VShe tried to ignore the sounds.
ADJECTIVES
Base adjectivesUse with: very, quite, rathervery/quite + base adjIt's very cold. She's quite tired.
Strong adjectivesUse with: absolutely, completely, totallyabsolutely + strong adjIt's absolutely freezing!
